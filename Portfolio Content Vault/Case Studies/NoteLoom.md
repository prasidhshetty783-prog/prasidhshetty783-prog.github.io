# NoteLoom — AI Notetaker

**Type:** Masters Project · **Cover:** `7xmcBBQALfX38yEYyZZWSvCUUig.png`

## Hero
**NoteLoom — AI Notetaker.** NoteLoom is an AI-supported study platform designed to help college students manage cognitive overload caused by fragmented study tools and fast-paced lectures. Over 10 weeks, my team and I researched, designed, and tested a solution that centralizes note-taking, summarization, and active recall. Through three rounds of iterative testing, we improved the System Usability Scale (SUS) score from **78.5 to 97.5**, creating a tool that balances AI automation with human agency.

## Meta
- Role: UX Researcher & Designer
- Industry: Education
- Team Size: 6 members
- Timeline: Oct 2025 – Dec 2025

## Problem Statement
College students today navigate a chaotic ecosystem of handwritten notes, PDF slides, and various AI tools. This fragmentation leads to high cognitive load and reduced learning efficiency.
- **Tool Overload** — Students juggle screenshots, slides, and separate AI tools (like ChatGPT), leading to scattered information.
- **Organization Debt** — Students lose significant time manually reorganizing material before they can even begin studying.
- **Speed vs Structure** — During fast-paced lectures, prioritizing capturing words over understanding, often resulting in messy, unusable notes.

## Secondary Research (18 articles → 4 insights) — img `FUnUBNB5IZCrX94kUJVjngs3mE.png`
1. **Multimodal Learning Accelerates Retention** — relying purely on massive blocks of text is inefficient; design must bridge text and visuals.
2. **"Explainable" and Controllable AI** — students are skeptical of "black-box" AI; NoteLoom had to be a transparent partner, not a Magic 8-Ball.
3. **Active Engagement vs Digital Distraction** — digital tools often become a source of distraction; keep students actively engaged.
4. **Cognitive Load Optimization** — overload and disorganization drive burnout; NoteLoom acts as an organizational anchor.

## 1-on-1 User Interviews (7 students, 18–35) — img `NLPMmfjFMpdNCEQQ0EXoUiGY0QY.png`
1. **Efficiency & Overload** — cognitive cost of reorganizing across apps exceeds the learning benefit.
2. **Speed vs Comprehension Deficit** — in fast lectures students abandon structured notes, capturing fragments lacking context.
3. **Trust & Transparency** — eager for AI time-savings but skeptical of automation that doesn't show its work.
4. **Stress Levels** — messy materials scattered across PDFs/screenshots/docs trigger anxiety.

## Prototype & Testing Phase
Mid-fi prototypes → first usability test (within-subject) → hi-fi iterations for later rounds.

**Round 1 — Mid-Fidelity:** validated dashboard structure + core note flow. Friction: (1) Accessibility — users struggled to title notes (input lacked visual boundaries); (2) Hidden AI features buried in nested menus.
→ **SUS 78.5 · 5 participants · 95% task completion**

**Round 2 — Hi-Fi v1:** color improved appeal; streamlined note creation worked. Friction: (1) Weak affordance editing flashcards/summaries; (2) Visual hierarchy of title section; (3) wanted more control (multiple flashcard formats, edit AI summaries).
→ **SUS 85.5 · 5 participants · 100% task completion**

**Round 3 — Hi-Fi v2:** validated refined interactions. Resolved: clear affordances (hint text fixed title confusion); surfaced AI (summaries/flashcards discoverable).
→ **SUS 97.5 · 5 participants · 100% task completion**

## High Fidelity Prototype
Video of the finalized prototype with all feedback incorporated.

## Key Findings
1. **Explicit Affordances are Non-Negotiable** — subtle cues failed with fast-paced users; clear boundaries + text nudges prevent task abandonment.
2. **Visibility Drives Adoption** — users relied on top-level features and avoided nested menus; surface high-value AI actions next to the editor.
3. **The Automation vs Control Tension** — keep students actively engaged rather than passively automated.

## AI Use in the Project
Usage: Crazy 8s idea generation · feature comparison · UI flow visualization.
AI-Teaming: "AI sped up layout ideas… but lacked empathy." "It sparked ideas, not decisions."
Takeaway: AI = divergent thinking (quantity); Humans = convergent decisions (quality); Empathy > automation.

## Conclusion
Through three rounds of iterative, human-centered design, NoteLoom evolved from concept into an intuitive, cognitively supportive study platform. By listening to user friction and responding with explicit interaction cues and surfaced navigation, NoteLoom turns a fragmented study process into a centralized, efficient workflow — using AI to reduce busywork while preserving transparency and user agency, reflected in the jump to a final **SUS of 97.5**.
