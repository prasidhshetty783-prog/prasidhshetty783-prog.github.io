Case Study — Professional Project

Media Production & Planning Tool

A media production management and planning tool that helps creators and providers plan, collaborate, manage, transact, and synchronize on a single platform.

Client
Showrunnr
Role
UX & UI Designer
Industry
Film & Production
Timeline
Aug 2024 — Jul 2025

01 Problem Statement

Scattered tools, misaligned productions

Media teams rely on scattered tools and manual processes, causing miscommunication, delays, and budget issues. They need a single platform that unifies planning, collaboration, management, and transactions so everyone stays aligned and productions run smoothly.

i.

Project Planning

Clear, organized planning that keeps every task and timeline in sync.

ii.

Team & Assets

A shared space where teams communicate and manage all production files.

iii.

Smart Transactions

Instant updates and smooth transactions to keep the entire workflow moving.

02 User Personas

Two sides of every production

There are primarily two main personas: Production and Supplier.

Cameron Williamson

Age 27 · Line Producer — Production Persona

Cameron juggles budgets, crews, and schedules but struggles with scattered details and last-minute changes.

Core Needs
  • Stay on top of multiple productions.
  • Track budgets, crew bookings, and schedules in one place.
  • Get hired for relevant projects.
  • Keep track of upcoming shoots, availability, and requirements.
Pain Points
  • Shoot details scattered across tools — Sheets, emails, texts.
  • Last-minute changes derail planning.
  • No centralized view of crew availability and budget status.
  • Uncertainty when juggling parallel projects.

Wade Warren

Age 40 · Owner, Set Lighting & Props Rental — Supplier Persona

Wade runs a rental business, aiming for smoother bookings but often slowed by poor coordination and late updates.

Core Needs
  • Marketplace listing tools for visibility.
  • Inquiry management & booking calendar in one dashboard.
  • Collaborative space with permission-based access.
  • Timeline overviews linked to productions.
Pain Points
  • Manual coordination through calls, emails, and spreadsheets.
  • Uncertain payment timelines hurt cash flow.
  • Miscommunication causes last-minute rushes.
  • Struggles to find legitimate leads early in the process.
  • Delayed updates on rescheduling or location changes.

03 From Pain Points to Progress

Two workflows, one platform

App i.

Production Planning

One place for people or companies creating or managing productions to track projects and connect with the resources & services they need.


— Keep track of important production needs, schedules & logistics.
— Track the resources you need.
— Share requirements with providers and collaborate.

App ii.

Reservation Management

One place for people or companies providing talent, resources, or services to track bookings and connect with productions that need them.


— End-to-end booking workflow.
— Centralized information for sharing and decision-making.
— Real-time visibility: stay in sync with production needs, leads, and bookings.

An evolving, centralized hub

The next phase will focus on refining features, user testing, and iterating toward a seamless, production-ready platform.
